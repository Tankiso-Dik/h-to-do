## Design Context

### Users
People using this app are planning personal daily work on mobile, often in quick, low-attention moments. They are trying to capture tasks fast, follow through realistically, and review what actually happened without carrying guilt-heavy backlog pressure.

### Brand Personality
Calm, observant, supportive. The interface should feel lightweight, steady, and emotionally safe rather than gamified, noisy, or pushy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Mobile-first minimalism inspired by the calm clarity of Microsoft To Do, but distilled further for darker, more skeletal task views. Favor alarm-app style dark mode surfaces: quiet separators, restrained contrast, simple rings, sparse metadata, and very little decorative framing. Avoid chunky cards, busy badges, excessive borders, and dense stacked helper text.

### Design Principles
1. Preserve calm before expressiveness.
2. Prefer spacing, rhythm, and separators over boxed containers.
3. Keep the primary action obvious and the supporting information compressed.
4. Treat missed or changed task states as neutral signals, never as visual punishment.
5. Make mobile rows glanceable first, detailed second.
